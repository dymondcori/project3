Drunk Driving in the United States

Drunk Driving is a serious problem in the United States contributing to over 10,000 deaths a year.

There are many factors that play into drinking and driving, such as age, gender, location, race and other demographics. Because states determine the laws and enforcement of the laws to keep drunk drivers off of roads, there is variation in DUI laws across the nation.

The map shows the levels of strictness of DUI law for each state relative to eachother.
1= most lenient and 51= most strict.

It is clear from the coloration on the map that states tend to be more strict in the South and Southwest and more lenient in the North East and North West.

Laws vary from state to state on charges and punishment for first, second, and third offenses. There are also different laws regarding the consequences for underage drinking and driving. The ranking system for each state's level of strictness takes these factors into consideration, along with other laws regarding drunk driving.

The following graph aranges states in order of their strictness ranking from most strict to least strict. It then compares their ranking for such as strictness of criminal penalty laws and implementation of prevention laws.

Party Connection

A states party alignment plays a crucial role in determining the strictness of their DUI laws. It is clear that the division of states between strictness of DUI laws is similarly divided by Republican and Democratic states.

On the map, Republican states are labeled RED and the Democrat states are BLUE.

Republican states are generally more strict than Democratic states.
